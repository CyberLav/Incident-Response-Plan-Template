# 6. Lessons Learned

**Objective:** Capture what happened and improve the plan. This phase is not optional and
should not be skipped once the pressure is off, which is exactly when it's most likely to
get skipped.

*Battle procedure step 16 — After Action Review.*

## 6.1 AAR Timing

Schedule within [5–10 business days] of incident closure. Close enough that memory is
fresh, far enough that people aren't still in triage mode.

## 6.2 AAR Structure

See [Annex G](annexes/G-aar-template.md) for the fillable template. Structure:

1. **What was supposed to happen?** (the plan, as written)
2. **What actually happened?** (the factual timeline, no blame language)
3. **Why was there a difference?** (root cause of any deviation such as process, tooling,
   people, or the plan itself was wrong)
4. **What will we sustain?** (things that worked and should not change)
5. **What will we improve?** (specific, owned, dated action items avoide "communication
   should be better")

## 6.3 Metrics Captured

| Metric | Definition |
|---|---|
| MTTD | Mean Time to Detect — Warning Order to formal activation |
| MTTC | Mean Time to Contain — activation to containment order issued |
| MTTE | Mean Time to Eradicate — containment to eradication sign-off |
| MTTR | Mean Time to Recover — eradication sign-off to incident closure |

## 6.4 Plan Update Cycle

- [ ] Action items from AAR assigned owners and due dates
- [ ] Plan and relevant [playbook](playbooks/) updated to reflect lessons learned
- [ ] Update logged in the repo's version history / CHANGELOG
